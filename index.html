Hifoens thoughts on games

This is written for my own sake, but if you find it useful, good. If not, just pretend you never saw it.

Intro

Throughout my life, especially during high school and university, I have spent a lot of time playing games. However, I eventually realized that games were just a way for me to cope with my perfectionism and real-life problems. I believe that while games can provide sense of love and belonging, esteem, and self-actualization, as defined in Maslow’s hierarchy of needs, they are not real - they are just a game.

Copilot: “I think games are a way for me to cope with my perfectionism. I have a lot of perfectionism. I want to be perfect, the best, the smartest, the most successful, popular, loved, respected, powerful, influential, rich, beautiful, healthy, fit, athletic, creative, productive, efficient, effective, organized, disciplined, consistent, persistent, patient, kind, generous, grateful, humble, honest, loyal, trustworthy, reliable, responsible, mature, wise, intelligent, knowledgeable, skillful, talented, innovative, resourceful, resilient, adaptable, flexible, versatile, open-minded, curious, adventurous, courageous, confident, optimistic, positive, happy, joyful, peaceful, calm, relaxed, and patient.”

While this statement may seem jumbled, it does have a point. After giving up video games, I realized that I had nothing left. I had given up my hopes of becoming a rocket scientist, a pentester, or a physicist, and I didn’t feel loved or a sense of belonging.

Greedy algorithm

I am no neuroscientist, but this is my view of the matter.

How video games suck you back in

Fear of Missing Out

  • New raids, time-limited events, limited skins, and limited operators create a fear of missing out.
  • While it may seem easy to avoid, the fear of missing out is all around us in the world. It’s very easy to get caught up in the scheme of FOMO.

Community

  • The people you know will suck you back in, the less people you know the less likely you will be sucked in
  • Otherwise known as social pressure
  • I am not against gaming as a tool for socializing, but once you realize that your mentality has become less fun-driven and more win-driven, it may be time to take a step back.

Sense of progression

  • Armor, gear, and weapons can make you feel like you have earned something.
  • Min-maxing can take up a lot of your time.

Becoming best of the best

  • I am torn on this topic, as competitive video games are similar to competitive sports and can provide job opportunities. Therefore, I believe that video games are heading in that direction. While I don’t think video games will disappear in the short term, I believe they will eventually become less popular, similar to certain sports today.

Is gaming the hill I want to die on? no. But your choices are yours.
